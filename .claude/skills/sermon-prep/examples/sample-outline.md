# Sample Sermon Outline

**Passage:** John 11:1-44
**Series:** Life and Death (Week 3)
**Date:** 2026-04-13
**Big Idea:** Jesus delays not because he is indifferent, but because he is after something bigger than comfort.

---

## Introduction

**Hook:** Tell me about a time a doctor made you wait and you were furious — then found out the wait was the treatment.

**Bridge:** Mary and Martha sent word to Jesus. He stayed where he was two more days. By the time he arrived, Lazarus had been dead four days. This passage is about that delay and what it means that Jesus chose it.

---

## Movement 1: The Delay Is Not an Oversight (vv. 1-16)

**Text:** "When he heard that Lazarus was sick, he stayed where he was two more days." (v. 6)

**Observation:** John tells us Jesus loved this family (v. 5) — then immediately tells us Jesus stayed put. The delay is not negligence. It is intentional.

**Application:** Most of us experience God's silence or slowness as absence. Jesus reframes it: the delay is not abandonment. He knows what he is doing.

---

## Movement 2: The Grief Is Not a Contradiction (vv. 17-37)

**Text:** "Jesus wept." (v. 35)

**Observation:** Jesus is about to raise a man from the dead. He knows this. He weeps anyway. This is not confusion. It is compassion. The God who controls the outcome still enters the grief.

**Key tension:** If Jesus knew Lazarus would live, why weep? Because resurrection does not make death not real. He grieves what death does to people he loves, even when he knows the ending.

**Application:** Your grief is not a sign of weak faith. Jesus wept at a grave he was about to empty.

---

## Movement 3: The Point Was Always Greater (vv. 38-44)

**Text:** "Did I not tell you that if you believe, you will see the glory of God?" (v. 40)

**Observation:** Martha wants her brother back. Jesus wants her to see who he is. Both happen — but in a different order than she expected.

**The turn:** This story is not about Lazarus. Lazarus dies again eventually. This story is about who Jesus is: the resurrection and the life. The miracle is the sign. The sign points somewhere.

**Application:** When God does not give you what you asked for on the schedule you asked for, the question is not whether he heard you. The question is what he is after that is bigger than what you asked.

---

## Conclusion

**Restate the big idea:** Jesus delays not because he is indifferent, but because he is after something bigger than comfort.

**Call to response:** Name one situation in your life where you have been waiting on God and interpreting the silence as absence. This week, pray into that specific thing — not for the answer you want, but for the trust that the one who wept at Lazarus' tomb is present in yours.

**Closing image:** The stone is rolled away before anyone inside knew it was coming.
