# Sermon Prep

Full workflow from passage to outline. One session, three phases.

**Requires:** pastor-foundation skill context

---

## What This Does

Walks you through the complete sermon prep process:

1. **Brainstorm** — clarify the big idea, tension, and desired response
2. **Research** — key exegetical notes, cross-references, and commentary touchpoints
3. **Outline** — a working sermon structure ready to develop into a manuscript

You drive. I ask questions, surface what you already know, and organize it into something you can preach.

---

## Church Context

Before starting, confirm or provide:

- Passage or topic
- Series (if applicable)
- Preaching date
- Anything specific to your congregation this week (see `references/demographics.md`)

---

## Phase 1: Brainstorm

One question at a time. We are looking for:

- The big idea (one sentence, subject + predicate)
- The key tension (where the text pushes back)
- The audience need (what your people are carrying)
- The desired response (what changes Monday morning)

See `sermon-prep/sermon-brainstorm/SKILL.md` for the full question sequence.

---

## Phase 2: Research

Once the brief is clear:

- Immediate and wider context of the passage
- Key word or phrase worth sitting with
- 2-3 cross-references that strengthen the message
- One interpretive caution (common misreading to avoid)
- One illustration seed

See `sermon-prep/sermon-research/SKILL.md` for the full research protocol.

---

## Phase 3: Outline

With brief and research in hand:

- Introduction (hook + bridge to passage)
- Body (2-4 movements, each grounded in the text)
- Conclusion (call to response)

See `references/theological-framework.md` for guardrails on structure and application.
See `examples/sample-outline.md` for a finished example.

---

## Output

A working sermon outline saved to `outlines/[date]-[passage-slug].md`.

Not a manuscript. Not a script. A structure you can preach.
